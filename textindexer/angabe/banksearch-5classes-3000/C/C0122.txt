Home Insurance - Contents cover policy
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Section 4b - Home legal 
 protection 
 
 
 This section only applies 
 to you when it is shown in the policy schedule. 
 Cover under this section is provided by Lawclub Legal 
 Protection (a trading division of Cornhill Insurance PLC). 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The 
 meaning of words 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 We, our, us 
 Lawclub Legal Protection - a division 
 of Cornhill Insurance PLC. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 You , your 
 
 the person named in the schedule and 
 his or her partner, children, parents and parents-in-law 
 who live permanently at the same address. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Legal representative 
 
 the solicitor, barrister or expert 
 appointed under this section to represent you and 
 protect your interests. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Costs 
 
 
under this section, we will pay the following;
 
 
- The professional fees and expenses reasonably and 
 properly charged by the legal representative, 
 up to the standard rates set by the courts. 
 
 
- The other person&#146;s costs which you are ordered 
 to pay by a court or tribunal. 
 
 
- The most we will pay for all claims arising 
 from one event is £25,000. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Partner 
 
 someone you are married to or 
 live with as if you were married. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Territorial 
 limits (for personal injury only) 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Great Britain, Northern Ireland, 
 the Channel Islands, the Isle of Man, Andorra, Austria, 
 Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, 
 Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Monaco, the Netherlands, Norway, 
 Portugal, the Republic of Ireland, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland 
 
 and Turkey. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Period 
 of insurance 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The period shown in the 
 schedule. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 What 
 is covered 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 If there is a reasonable 
 chance of recovering damages or another way of carrying 
 out a successful defence, and the event which gave rise 
 to the claim happens within the period of insurance, 
 we will pay the costs if you or your 
 representatives take legal action against another person 
 as a result of the following. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 For personal 
 injury 
 An event which caused your death 
 or injury and which happens in the territorial limits. 
 You must also take any action arising from the 
 event within the territorial limits. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 For a customer 
 contract 
 A dispute over a contract you 
 have for buying, selling or renting goods or services. 
 You must have entered into the contract and taken 
 legal action within Great Britain, Northern Ireland, the 
 Channel Islands or the Isle of Man. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 What 
 is not covered 
 
 
 
 We 
 will not provide cover for the following. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 1 
 
 The first £50 of every claim. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 2 
 
 Any claim relating to you 
 driving a motor vehicle. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 3 
 
 Any dispute about a contract 
 that happens within three months of the date 
 this section starts, unless the claim is for 
 goods or services you bought after 
 the start of this section. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 4 
 
 Any dispute about a contract 
 if the amount in dispute is less than £250 
 (or less than £1,000 for home improvements). 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 5 
 
 Any contract under which 
 money was owed more than six months before 
 you reported the claim. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 6 
 
 Any contract to do with motor 
 vehicles or their parts or accessories. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 7 
 
 Any contract to do with building, 
 converting or extending your home. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 8 
 
 Any contract to do with work 
 carried out on land or buildings that are 
 not your permanent home. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 9 
 
 A dispute over the amount 
 of money or other compensation owed to you 
 under any insurance policy. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 10 
 
 Anything to do with landlord 
 and tenancy agreements. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 11 
 
 Anything to do with a contract 
 for your business activities. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 12 
 
 Any costs covered 
 by another insurance policy. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 13 
 
 Disputes between you 
 and us, unless they are dealt with 
 under condition 6. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 14 
 
 Disputes between you 
 and Cornhill Insurance PLC, including trading 
 divisions. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 15 
 
 Disputes between you 
 and AON Risk Services Ltd. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 16 
 
 Costs we have not 
 agreed to in writing. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 17 
 
 Disputes between you, 
 your partner, children, parents and 
 parents-in-law. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 18 
 
 Costs you have paid 
 directly to the legal representative 
 or any other person without our permission. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 19 
 
 Any VAT which you 
 have to get back from elsewhere. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 20 
 
 
 
Claims directly or indirectly caused by, 
 contributed to or arising from:
 
 
- ionising radiation or radioactive contamination 
 from nuclear fuel or from any nuclear 
 waste arising from burning nuclear fuel; 
 or
 
- the radioactive, toxic, explosive or 
 other dangerous properties of any nuclear 
 equipment or nuclear part of that equipment.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 21 
 
 Claims arising from war, 
 invasion, riot, revolution or a similar event. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 22 
 
 Cover for any claim directly 
 or indirectly caused by or resulting from 
 any device, (whoever owns it), failing to 
 recognise, interpret or process any date as 
 its true calendar date. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Conditions 
 (applying to section 4b only) 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 If you do not keep 
 to the conditions, we may cancel the section and 
 refuse any claim and withdraw from any current claim. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 1) 
 You must do the following: 
 
 
 
 
 
 a) Give 
 us written details of your claim 
 along with any other supporting information we 
 ask for. 
 b) Make your claim within six months of 
 the event which gave rise to the dispute.
 c) Follow the legal representatives's advice 
 and give any information he or she asks for.
 d) Take every step to get back costs and 
 pay them to us. 
 e) Get our written permission before making 
 an appeal. 
 f) Make sure that your legal representative 
 keeps to condition 2 below. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 2) 
 Your legal representative 
 must do the following: 
 
 
 
 
 a) Get 
 our written permission before instructing 
 a barrister or expert witness. 
 b) Tell us if, at any stage, there is no 
 longer a reasonable chance of a successful defence, 
 getting back damages or any other form of successful 
 defence. 
 c) Tell us immediately if the other person 
 makes a payment into court or about any offers 
 to settle the matter. 
 d) Report the result of the claim to us 
 when it is finished.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 3) 
 We will have the right to 
 do the following: 
 
 
 
 
 a) Take 
 over and carry out, in your name, any claim 
 or legal proceedings.
 b) Settle a claim by paying the amount in dispute. 
 
 c) Appoint the legal representative in 
 your name and for you. 
 d) Have any legal bill audited or taxed. 
 e) Contact the legal representative at 
 any time, and have access to all statements, opinions 
 and reports. 
 f) End your cover if, during the course 
 of the claim, we think there is no longer 
 a reasonable chance of success. If you 
 continue the claim and get a better settlement 
 than we expected, we will pay your 
 reasonable costs which you cannot 
 get back from anywhere else. 
 g) At the end of the claim, we will settle 
 the costs covered by this section if you 
 cannot get those costs back from anywhere 
 else.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 4) 
 Your agreement with others. 
 
 
 
 
 We will not have to keep to any agreement 
 between you and the legal representative, 
 or you and any other person or organisation. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 5) 
 Choosing the legal representative. 
 
 
 
 
 When you need to start legal proceedings 
 you can choose the legal representative. 
 You must send his or her name and address 
 to us. If we do not agree with your 
 choice, we will settle the matter as set 
 out under condition 6 below. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 6) 
 Disputes. 
 
 
 
 
 If there is a dispute between you and us, 
 the matter may be referred to an arbitrator who 
 you and we agree to. 
 
 If either of us cannot agree on an arbitrator, 
 the President of the Law Society or the Bar Council 
 will choose one.
 
 All costs of the arbitration will be paid 
 by the person the arbitrator&#146;s decision goes against. 
 If the decision is not clearly made against either 
 you or us, the arbitrator will decide 
 how you and we will share the costs 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 7) 
 Cancellation. 
 
 
 
 
 We or you may cancel this section 
 by giving seven days&#146; written notice. Cancelling 
 the section will not affect your right to 
 claim for an event which happened before the date 
 the section was cancelled. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 8) 
 Notices. 
 
 
 
 
 Every notice or other correspondence must be made 
 in writing by:
 
 a) you to:
 
 Lawclub Legal Protection
 Redwood House
 Brotherswood Court
 Great Park Road
 Bradley Stoke 
 Bristol 
 BS32 4QW
 
 
 or 
 
 b) us to you at your last 
 known address. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 9) 
 This policy will be governed 
 by English Law.
