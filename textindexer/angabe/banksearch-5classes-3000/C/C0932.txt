Who
 Should Attend the Lloyd's Introductory Test Course?
 
 
 
 
 
Those who work in Lloyd's, or communicate via
 electronic placing support (EPS) who are required by Lloyd's byelaw to
 pass the Lloyd's Introductory Test.
 
 
 
 
 
This qualification is also extremely useful for
 support staff who would benefit from a better understanding of the
 Lloyd's market.
 
 
 
 
 
This is a revision course which is designed to meet
 the needs of people who are actively studying towards the LIT. To meet
 the needs of others, BIBA has a number of technical introductory courses
 which can form part of an induction programme.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Course Objectives
 
 
 
 
 
 
 To
 consolidate and retain knowledge gained from the book An
 Introduction to Lloyd's
 
 
 
 
 
 
 To
 assist delegates with their final revision and preparation for the test
 and help achieve success
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Pre-course
 Study
 
 
 
 
 
 
 To
 obtain full benefit from this course it is necessary for delegates to
 have studied the text book prior
 to attendance
 
 
 
 
 
 
 This
 course is in addition to, not a replacement for a student's own studies
 
 
 
Contents
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The
 course will follow the sequence of study set out in the book which forms
 the basis for all examinations questions
 
 
 
 Successful examination techniques will be
 studied
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Approach
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Delegates
 will take mini 'mock' tests upon completion of each of the ten sections
 of the course. There will also be a full 'mock' test at the end. These
 tests enable delegates to monitor and discuss their progress with the
 tutor
 
 
 
 
 
Class
 Size
 
 
 
 
 
 
 To
 ensure adequate individual attention the class will be limited to 14
 delegates
 
 
 
 
 
Fees
 
 
 
 
 The course fee is £200 per person for BIBA members
 and £280 per person for non-members. 
 The fee includes lunch and coffee breaks, but does not include
 exam registration for the LIT
