 Key Dates For Euro Chageover 
 
 
 
 
KEY DATES FOR &euro;URO CHANGEOVER
 
 
 
 
31 December 2001 - Last date for payments in legacy currency.
 
 
 
 
September 2001 onwards - Consider purchasing euro denominated drafts instead of legacy currency.
 
31 December 2001 - This is the last day that you can purchase drafts in legacy currency.
 
 
 
 
01 January 2002 - All legacy accounts held in Bank of Ireland will have been converted to euro.
 
 
 
 
31 December 2001 - This is the last day that Bank of Ireland will sell legacy currency cash.
 
02 January 2002 - Euro notes will be available from NI branches and can also be ordered from Great Britain branches.
(Consider ordering in advance to avoid disappointment).
 
15 February 2002 - This is the last day that Bank of Ireland will accept legacy currency cash.
 
 
 
September 2001 onwards - euro travellers cheques are available instead of legacy currency.
 
15 February 2002 - This is the last day that Bank of Ireland will accept legacy currency cheques, drafts and travellers cheques for Customers. 
 
16 February 2002 onwards - Legacy currency cheques may be sent for Special Collection, value will be subject to Paying Bank, normal charges apply.
