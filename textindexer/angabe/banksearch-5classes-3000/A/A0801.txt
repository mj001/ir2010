Business First - Spring 2002
The latest issue of Business First, the magazine produced by The Royal Bank 
of Scotland for senior business managers in the mid-corporate sector is now 
available.
You can download the entire issue or order a print version from Teresa
Fitzgibbons. 
This issue features the following articles:
Business focus &#150; Coverage of upcoming events, sources of help and industry news
The euro and you &#150; Richard Chalk and Jeremy Peat of The Royal Bank of Scotland consider the implications of the introduction of euro notes and coins
Path to creativity &#150; Profile customer John Varney and his Centre for Creative Management
Identify yourself &#150; When you create a brand, the goal is to get your customers to like it and retain a lasting relationship with it &#150; we give you some practical advice on where to start
Cut out the middleman &#150; Founder of easyJet and easyGroup Stelios Haji-Ioannou tells us about the best business decision of his career
Personal best &#150; Coutt&#146;s Wealth Management services are perfect for busy executives who have little time to keep an eye on their personal finances
A bridge to success &#150; Adrian Critchlow and his Relationship Manager, Paul Marks, discuss their partnership
Refresh your finances &#150; Financial management can be extremely daunting, but the Bank can offer a few solutions to make it much easier
Walk this way &#150; Making people redundant is a sensitive matter that must be handled with tact, as well as ensuring that you stay within the law
Three of a kind &#150; Payment issues can be a major cause of stress for companies involved in trade. Here, the Bank provides three solutions through trade finance
Chequed out &#150; Research shows that cheque use is in decline. With automated payments more popular than ever, the decline may be terminal 
Economic focus &#150; Group Chief Economist Jeremy Peat comments on the global economy
In contact &#150; Useful resources for your business
