Partnerships and sponsorships
 
Barclays participates in - and helps to 
 sponsor - many external strategic groups and networks concerned 
 with environmental management. We do this both to learn and 
 to contribute - for example, by providing speakers at seminars. 
 We are committed to a number of external bodies, programmes 
 and projects, including: 
 
 
- Business 
 and the Environment - corporate sponsors and participants 
 in the training programme for senior executives developed 
 by the University of Cambridge Programme for Industry 
 
- British Bankers' Association - founder of and ongoing commitment to the Environmental Issues Advisory Panel and the Corporate Social Responsibility Advisory Panel
 
- Business in the Environment - active participation by Barclays Chairman and Environmental Director in the Leadership Team and Practitioners' Forum respectively
 
- Earthwatch Institute - founder member of the Corporate Environmental Responsibility Group
 
- International Chamber of Commerce - signatory to the Business Charter for Sustainable Development
 
- Making A Corporate Commitment 2 - Barclays is a signatory to this Government-sponsored scheme. This commits us to improvement in the areas of CO2 emissions (relating to electricity and gas usage and business travel) and water consumption. Each year we report our progress in meeting our targets to the Government (download report [23k Microsoft Word document])
 
- The Forge Group - participant in joint government-industry initiative to provide guidance on environmental management and reporting and in the near future on Corporate Social Responsibility, specifically for the financial services sector
 
- UN Environment Programme - signatory to the financial institutions' statement on the environment and sustainable development, and active member of the Steering Committee
 
- WWF-UK - Sustainability Action Network - an active participant in this Network of individuals, not-for-profit organisations and businesses, to explore innovations and solutions for corporate sustainability that can - on a practical basis - be adopted by business
 
- Young Environmentalist of the Year Awards 2001 - one of a number of companies running competitions for employees' children (UK based).
 
- Digital Europe - Corporate Sponsors of this flagship pan-European study into the social and environmental effects of ebusiness and information communication technology
 
- Confederation of British Industry - Ongoing commitment to the Environmental Affairs Committee and the Environmental Liability Working Group
 
- Institute of Environmental Management and Assessment (IEMA) - active participant in the Institute's professional standards panels
